# Summary: Introduction to Algorithms, Third Edition

**Introduction to Algorithms, Third Edition** is a comprehensive textbook authored by Thomas H. Cormen, Charles E. Leiserson, Ronald L. Rivest, and Clifford Stein, published by MIT Press in 2009.

## Structure and Content

The book is organized into eight major parts covering the breadth of algorithm design and analysis:

**Part I: Foundations** - Introduces algorithms, their role in computing, and fundamental analysis techniques including asymptotic notation, [[divide-and-conquer|divide-and-conquer]] strategies, and [[probabilistic-analysis|probabilistic analysis]] with [[randomized-algorithms|randomized algorithms]].

**Part II: [[sorting-and-order-statistics|Sorting and Order Statistics]]** - Covers essential sorting algorithms including heapsort, quicksort, and linear-time sorting methods (counting sort, radix sort, bucket sort), plus selection algorithms for finding [[medians-and-order-statistics|medians and order statistics]].

**Part III: Data Structures** - Examines elementary structures (stacks, queues, linked lists), hash tables, binary search trees, red-black trees, and techniques for augmenting data structures.

**Part IV: Advanced Design and Analysis Techniques** - Explores three major algorithmic paradigms: [[dynamic-programming|dynamic programming]], [[greedy-algorithms|greedy algorithms]], and amortized analysis.

**Part V: Advanced Data Structures** - Presents sophisticated structures including B-trees, Fibonacci heaps, [[van-emde-boas-trees|van Emde Boas trees]], and data structures for disjoint sets.

**Part VI: Graph Algorithms** - Covers graph representations, traversal algorithms (BFS, DFS), [[minimum-spanning-trees|minimum spanning trees]], shortest path algorithms (Bellman-Ford, Dijkstra's, Floyd-Warshall), and maximum flow problems.

**Part VII: Selected Topics** - Addresses specialized areas including [[multithreaded-algorithms|multithreaded algorithms]], matrix operations, [[linear-programming|linear programming]], polynomials and FFT, [[number-theoretic-algorithms|number-theoretic algorithms]], string matching, [[computational-geometry|computational geometry]], NP-completeness theory, and approximation algorithms.

**Part VIII: Mathematical Appendix** - Provides background on summations, sets, relations, and functions.

This textbook serves as a foundational reference for computer science education, covering both theoretical foundations and practical algorithm implementation.
